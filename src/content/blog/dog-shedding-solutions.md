## Why Do Dogs Shed?

### Normal Shedding
- Seasonal coat changes (spring and fall)
- Natural hair growth cycle
- Breed characteristics

### Excessive Shedding Causes
- Poor nutrition
- Allergies
- Stress or anxiety
- Parasites (fleas, mites)
- Skin infections
- Hormonal imbalances
- Underlying health conditions

## Heavy Shedding Breeds

- German Shepherds
- Labrador Retrievers
- Golden Retrievers
- Huskies
- Saint Bernards
- Akitas
- Chow Chows

## Low-Shedding Breeds

- Poodles
- Bichon Frises
- Maltese
- Yorkshire Terriers
- Shih Tzus
- Portuguese Water Dogs

## Solutions for Managing Shedding

### Regular Brushing
The most effective solution! Brush:
- Daily for heavy shedders
- 2-3 times weekly for moderate shedders
- Weekly for low shedders

### Proper Nutrition
Feed high-quality food with:
- Omega-3 and Omega-6 fatty acids
- Adequate protein
- Essential vitamins and minerals

### Regular Bathing
Monthly baths with deshedding shampoo help loosen dead fur.

### Stay Hydrated
Dehydrated skin leads to more shedding. Always provide fresh water.

### Professional Deshedding Treatments
Professional grooming can remove significant amounts of loose fur in one session.

## When to See a Vet

Consult your vet if shedding is accompanied by:
- Bald patches
- Skin irritation or redness
- Excessive scratching
- Changes in behavior or appetite

## Home Cleaning Tips

- Use furniture covers
- Invest in a quality pet hair vacuum
- Keep lint rollers handy
- Wash pet bedding weekly
