Dog Anal Gland Problems: Signs, Causes, and Solutions

Lillian Ruff Pet Spa

October 21, 2024
#anal glands #health issues #veterinary

Anal glands are small sacs located on either side of your dog’s anus. While not a pleasant topic, understanding these glands is important for your dog’s health and comfort.

What Are Anal Glands?

Anal glands produce a smelly, oily substance used for:

  • Scent marking
  • Communication with other dogs
  • Lubrication during defecation

In healthy dogs, the glands naturally express during bowel movements.

Signs of Anal Gland Problems

Watch for these symptoms:

  • Scooting: Dragging bottom across the floor
  • Excessive licking of the rear area
  • Difficulty sitting or reluctance to sit
  • Chasing or biting at the tail
  • Swelling near the anus
  • Foul, fishy odor
  • Blood or pus near the anus
  • Straining during bowel movements

Common Anal Gland Issues

Impaction

Glands become full and unable to empty naturally.

  • Most common problem
  • Causes discomfort and scooting
  • Requires manual expression

Infection

Bacteria enters the glands, causing:

  • Swelling and pain
  • Discharge
  • Fever in severe cases
  • Requires veterinary treatment

Abscess

Untreated infection leads to:

  • Severe swelling
  • Rupture through the skin
  • Significant pain
  • Emergency veterinary care needed

Tumors

Rare but serious:

  • Usually affects older dogs
  • May not be immediately obvious
  • Requires veterinary evaluation

Why Do Problems Occur?

Contributing Factors

  • Soft stools (don’t express glands naturally)
  • Obesity
  • Food allergies
  • Lack of fiber in diet
  • Anatomical abnormalities
  • Chronic skin conditions

Prone Breeds

Small breeds are most commonly affected:

  • Chihuahuas
  • Toy and Miniature Poodles
  • Cocker Spaniels
  • Basset Hounds
  • Beagles

Treatment Options

Professional Expression

  • Performed by groomers or vet staff
  • External or internal technique
  • Provides immediate relief
  • May need to be done regularly

Veterinary Treatment

For infections and abscesses:

  • Antibiotics
  • Anti-inflammatory medication
  • Flushing of the glands
  • Possible surgical drainage

Surgical Removal

For chronic, recurring problems:

  • Permanent solution
  • Removes both glands
  • Reserved for severe cases

Prevention Tips

  1. High-fiber diet promotes firm stools
  2. Maintain healthy weight
  3. Regular exercise
  4. Address allergies
  5. Regular grooming includes gland checks

When to See a Vet

Seek veterinary care if you notice:

  • Blood or pus
  • Severe swelling
  • Your dog seems in pain
  • Symptoms don’t improve with expression
  • Recurring issues
